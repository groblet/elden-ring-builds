⚠️ Critical Warnings & Tips
⚠️ WARNING: The Rakshasa set increases damage dealt but also increases damage received (2% per piece, both ways). Activate your Flask of Wondrous Physick before engaging enemies to compensate.
💡 TIP: Always two-hand the Greataxe in combat. The Two-Handed Sword Talisman specifically boosts two-handed attacks — you lose significant damage by one-handing.
💡 TIP: Cast both incantations before entering a fight. They do not stack with certain other buffs, so plan your buff order carefully.
⚠️ WARNING: Mind is set to 9 (base for Hero). You have very limited FP — use it only for pre-fight buffs, not mid-combat. Manage resources carefully.
💡 TIP: In PvP, the Blade of Mercy talisman rewards landing critical hits — riposte off a parry, or punish a whiffed roll with a backstab. Plan your openings around scoring that first crit to unlock the 20% buff window.
🧮 Phase 1: Stat Allocation (Target RL125)
Goal: Reach the exact stat spread for maximum efficiency at level 125.
Vigor → 60 ❤️ — Mandatory. Provides the HP cushion needed given the Rakshasa set's damage-received penalty.
Mind → 9 — Leave at base. You only need enough FP to cast two incantations pre-fight.
Endurance → 25 🏃 — Enough to wear the Rakshasa set without overloading, and maintain solid stamina for trades.
Strength → 58 💪 — Primary damage stat. Two-handing the Greataxe applies a ×1.5 multiplier, making your effective Strength 87 for scaling purposes.
Dexterity → 9 — Leave at base. This build uses Fire affinity, which removes Dex scaling.
Intelligence → 7 — Leave at base. Not relevant to this build.
Faith → 25 ✨ — Required to cast both incantations and use the Dragon Communion Seal effectively.
Arcane → 9 — Light investment; not a core scaling stat for this build's spell loadout.
Expected Outcome: A balanced spread that hits the 60 Vigor survival benchmark, 87 effective Strength, and 25 Faith for full incantation access — all at exactly RL125.
⚔️ Phase 2: Weapon Setup
Goal: Bring the Executioner's Greataxe to maximum upgrade and correct affinity.
Step 1 — Obtain the Executioner's Greataxe
Dropped by axe-wielding Skeleton enemies, roughly a 4% chance per kill.
Reliable farm spots: a Skeleton spawns in a narrow crevice near the Church of the Plague in Caelid (northeast of the church, between fallen stone arches); another spawns in the graveyard above Academy Gate Town, reachable from the South Raya Lucaria Gate Site of Grace; a third spawns in a graveyard west of Church of Irith in Liurnia of the Lakes.
Given the low drop rate, equip Gold Scarab talisman or use Silver-Pickled Fowl Foot to boost item discovery while farming.
Step 2 — Apply Fire Affinity 🔥
Locate a Whetstone Knife (found in Limgrave, Gatefront Ruins) to unlock Ash of War application.
Visit any Site of Grace and apply Fire affinity to the Executioner's Greataxe.
This converts the weapon's scaling to Strength + Faith (Fire), enabling synergy with your Faith investment and fire-boosting talismans.
Step 3 — Upgrade to +25
Use Smithing Stones (1 through 8) to upgrade incrementally.
Apply the Ancient Dragon Smithing Stone for the final +25 upgrade.
Expected AR at +25: ~378 Physical / ~394 Fire = ~772 combined AR before buffs.
Step 4 — Left Hand: Dragon Communion Seal +10
Upgrade the Dragon Communion Seal to +10 using Somber Smithing Stones.
Equip it in your left hand solely as a casting tool — you won't attack with it.
Found at Fringefolk Hero's Grave, Limgrave (use two Stonesword Keys on the double Imp Keystone next to the Stranded Graveyard Site of Grace).

How to obtain:
Defeat the Rakshasa boss (a Great Katana-wielding enemy) in the Eastern Nameless Mausoleum, Shadow of the Erdtree DLC.
Reach it from the Castle Watering Hole Site of Grace in the Land of Shadow: head south to the Recluses' River Downstream Site of Grace (past a Furnace Golem), then drop down the cliffside platforms heading northeast until you reach the mausoleum.
Defeating Rakshasa awards all four armor pieces plus Rakshasa's Great Katana in one drop.
⚠️ WARNING: Each piece of the Rakshasa set increases damage dealt by 2% and damage received by 2% (up to +8%/+8% for the full set). This is a meaningful DPS increase, but you become squishier — your 60 Vigor and Physick mitigate this tradeoff.
Expected Outcome: Mid-weight armour set providing the Rakshasa passive damage bonus across all four slots, keeping you in medium load for optimal roll speed.

🧪 Phase 5: Flask of Wondrous Physick
Goal: Configure your Physick for pre-fight survivability and sustained DPS.
Opaline Hardtear — Provides a one-time damage negation buff when consumed. Counters the physical damage-taken penalty from the Fire Scorpion Charm and the general damage-received increase from the Rakshasa set.
Spiked Cracked Tear — Boosts charged attacks for a duration after consumption. Pairs perfectly with Greataxe heavy/charged attacks.
How to use:
Before entering a fight, drink your Flask of Wondrous Physick.
Cast Flame, Grant Me Strength (raises Fire and Physical damage).
Cast Golden Vow (raises Attack and Defence).
Two-hand your Greataxe and engage.
💡 TIP: This buff window is time-limited. Engage immediately after buffing — don't waste the duration repositioning.
Expected Outcome: A fully buffed character with damage negation, increased fire/physical AR, boosted attack rating, and empowered charged attacks — all active simultaneously.
✨ Phase 6: Incantation Setup
Goal: Configure both support incantations for your pre-fight buff rotation.
Incantation 1 — Flame, Grant Me Strength 🔥
Raises Fire attack power and Physical attack power by 20% for 30 seconds (39 seconds with Old Lord's Talisman).
Obtained from a corpse behind Fort Gael, Caelid (between two Flame Chariots).
Requires Faith 15 to cast.
Stacks multiplicatively with Golden Vow.
Incantation 2 — Golden Vow 🌟
Raises attack power by 15% and damage negation by 10% for you and nearby allies, for 80 seconds.
Obtained from Corpse-Stench Shack, Mt. Gelmir.
Requires Faith 25 to cast.
Casting order (always follow this sequence):
Drink Physick (Opaline + Spiked tears activate).
Cast Flame, Grant Me Strength.
Cast Golden Vow.
Swap to Greataxe and two-hand it.
Engage.
⚠️ WARNING: Both spells cost FP. With Mind at 9, you have minimal FP to spare. Do not attempt to cast anything else mid-fight — your FP pool is exhausted after these two buffs.
Expected Outcome: Post-buff, your Executioner's Greataxe should output significantly more than its ~772 base AR, with enhanced physical and fire damage active simultaneously. Check your in-game stats screen with buffs active to confirm your exact numbers.
🗡️ Phase 7: Combat Approach
Goal: Execute the build effectively in both PvE and PvP scenarios.
PvE Playstyle 🛡️
Open every encounter with the full buff rotation (Phase 6 order).
Favour heavy attacks and charged R2s to proc the Spiked Cracked Tear bonus.
Use jump attacks to break enemy poise rapidly — Greataxes have exceptional poise damage.
Reposition to land backstabs and ripostes when safe — Dagger Talisman makes criticals hit extremely hard.
Re-drink Physick between major encounters to reset Opaline Hardtear protection.
PvP Playstyle ⚔️
Buff before the fight starts — opponents will not always give you time to cast.
Look for openings to land a critical hit (riposte off a parry, backstab a whiff) to trigger Blade of Mercy's 20-second attack power window.
Mix running attacks, jump attacks, and rolls into R1s to keep opponents guessing.
Heavy attacks are powerful but telegraphed — use them to punish whiffed rolls or after a stagger.
Respect the Fire Scorpion Charm penalty — prolonged trades against physical damage can be punishing (15% taken in PvP). Be aggressive, not reckless.
