# PREFACE (please read)

**DLC: These builds are updated to feature new equipment and all available upgrades from**

the Shadow of the Erdtree expansion.

**Basic stats: All builds feature 60 vigor, 51 poise, 16 endurance and a medium roll under the**

heaviest loadout. Capping the builds at level 125 was consciously chosen to strip them down to the essentials, avoid locking you out of any multiplayer activity, and leave room for your personal adaptations should you choose to go to level 139, 150, 164 or higher.

**Loadout: Some builds will appear as Overloaded or Heavy Load with the default loadout. If**

so, the unupgraded +0 weapon (e.g. Commander's Standard) is only meant to be used for its buff, then to be unequipped afterwards. This does not apply to weapons with Endure and Sacred Order.

**Buffs are the most efficient way to increase your damage (and/or negations). Elden Ring**

features an inflationary amount of buffs that can be stacked to increase their effect. However, excessive buff stacking takes a long time, requires planning, a lot of menu hardswaps, and the effects will only stay active for a limited time. The following builds require no buffstacking,

***no talisman hardswaps and feature only a reasonable amount of buffs. They do not rely on***

the use of consumables that are limited or time-intensive to farm: no Arteria Leaves (singular exception: Falx Tank), only Boiled Crab (infinitely purchaseable).

**Armor: Fashion is subjective and damage isn't. The armor on the builds is optimized for**

maximum negation at minimum weight while meeting the 51 poise breakpoint and including all relevant passives. The base builds (credit to unlined-betters) show off some of the best weapons, skills, spells, talismans and armor for each attribute.
